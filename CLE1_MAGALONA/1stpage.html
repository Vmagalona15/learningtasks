Definition of the issue

Corruption

- Poverty is defined as a lack of money and productive resources necessary for long-term survival.

-Absolute and relative poverty are two of the most common methods to define poverty. Absolute poverty refers to a lack of funds to meet fundamental needs such as food and shelter. The term "relative poverty" refers to how poor you are in comparison to other individuals.

- Hunger and malnutrition, restricted access to education and other basic services, social prejudice and marginalization, and a lack of participation in decision-making are examples of its expressions.

- a person who lacks a sufficient amount of money or material belongings to be considered normal or socially acceptable.

Problem

Corruption in the Philippines

The Philippines has high levels of population growth during crisis periods; high and persistent levels of inequality (incomes and assets), which reduce the positive effects of economic expansion; and economic crisis, conflicts, natural catastrophes, and "environmental poverty" are all examples of recurring shocks and exposure to hazards.

According to statistics, 16.6% of the population of the Philippines, or roughly 17.6 million people, live in poverty. People who live in rural areas are significantly more likely to be poor, with roughly one-third of those living in poverty working as farmers.
